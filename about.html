Bio

Arese Sylvester is a brilliant writer, editor, and storyteller from West Africa, Nigeria. She explores and tells the stories of marginalized demographics while documenting lifestyle, culture, and politics through her vast experiences. Some of her best work can be found at The Ayamba, Black Ballad, The Femme Magazine, Refinery29, openDemocracy and Reader's Digest. She currently works at The Nollywood Reporter as a senior writer.

In March 2022, openDemocracy published Arese’s article on the unfair treatment Nigerian students fleeing Ukraine had to face. The article gained a lot of traction internationally and helped shed a spotlight on the other side of the Russian-Ukraine war.

Arese currently lives in Uyo, Nigeria.

Monologue

“My Name is Arese Sylvester and I'm a Nigerian Journalist. I've been doing this for about a year and it's been very exhilarating. I work on a lot of projects and I often tell stories that explore the human mind, the intersection of feminism, mental health, relationships and lots more. I especially love it when I get to explore minority issues; typically issues that affect Nigerians. I love it when I get to tell the stories of Nigerians to a wider audience because, like I said, I'm Nigerian, and I think the Nigerian story is one of perseverance and resisting odds that are constantly being stacked against us. If you think about it, that's very terrific, and I think more people should know about it.”

preferred pronouns: she/her
